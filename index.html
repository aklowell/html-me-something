Quality Early Education Matters!

The SouthSide Spark

Working at SouthSide Early Childhood Center motivated me to dedicate my work and volunteer time to the issue of early care and education. SouthSide, as it is known, is a great program that serves diverse, multi-lingual, primarily low-income families with very high-quality and affordable full-day infant care and preschool. But it is a drop in the bucket compared to the need in St. Louis. While many excellent programs exist in the City, they serve only a fraction of families seeking quality child care that they can afford.

Here's a fun fact about SouthSide: it was founded (with the name South Side Day Nursery) in 1886 by 15 Unitarian women and is the oldest early childhood center in the state of Missouri! These women might look stern, but they had big hearts. Check out the smocks that teachers wore in the picture from the 1950s.

Quality for All Children

A dedicated group of volunteers in St. Louis, representing dozens of organizations that serve young children, is working to build a regional system that supports all families and children, especially those who lack access to quality early care and education and other services. The St. Louis Regional Early Childhood Council advocates for issues at the state and local level, and is working to create collaborative, comprehensive approaches to the challenges we face. RECC is a data-driven group. With a starting point of child well-being measures collected in 2013, RECC will continue to monitor the region's progress in meeting the needs of children and families most in need. One alarming statistic is the shortage of quality early care and education slots in our City. What You Can Do

There are a lot of things that we can do to help make sure that all St. Louis children have access to quality, affordable early care and edcuation! Here are just a few things you can do.

  • Learn about the issue and how it affects your community. Here are some great sites to visit:
    • The Heckman Equation: A Nobel Prize-winning economist shows how investing in early care and education pays off for families and communities.
    • Women's Foundation of St. Louis Report: Read what St. Louis women have to say about the challenges of finding quality, affordable care for their children.

  • Volunteer in your community
    • Ask the child care centers in your neighborhood how you can help! They might be looking for volunteers to read to children, or they might need help acquiring quality toys and materials. You can find a list of child care homes and centers at Child Care Aware of Missouri.
    • Many child care centers have volunteer days during the United Way Campaign in September and October. Ask at your workplace if you can organize one! If your company is not connected to the United Way Campaign, find out how to join at the United Way website.

  • Help build a strong regional support system for children and families!
    • Join the St. Louis Regional Early Childhood Council (RECC) and help ensure that all children have the chance to reach their full potential. RECC Committees include:
      • - Public Awareness & Advocacy
      - Early Care & Education
      - Health & Mental Health
      - Family & Community Engagement
      - Data
